🧬  Mito Research
  • A systematic review and meta-analysis published June 10, 2026 in Orphanet Journal of Rare Diseases synthesized 13 studies covering 988 MELAS patients to characterize the disease's genetic, clinical, and neuroimaging profile. Pooled results: m.3243A>G present in 78%; stroke-like episodes in 99%, seizures in 89%, elevated lactate in 81%. The temporal lobe was the most commonly affected brain region. Pathway analysis identified MNRR1, L-arginine, PI3K-AKT-mTOR, and the electron transport chain as the most promising targets for multi-targeted drug development — a framework the authors say could guide the next generation of MELAS treatments. (Based on articles retrieved from PubMed, PMID 42271506 — Orphanet J Rare Dis, doi:10.1186/s13023-026-04448-6)
  • A comprehensive review published June 2, 2026 in Cell Reports Medicine by researchers at UCL Queen Square Institute of Neurology and the MRC Mitochondrial Biology Unit (Cambridge) surveys the current therapeutic landscape for mtDNA-related diseases. Covered approaches include: allotopic expression, mitochondria-targeted nucleases, next-generation base editors capable of correcting specific mtDNA point mutations (including m.3243A>G), pharmacological heteroplasmy modulation, mitochondrial replacement therapy, and mitochondrial transplantation. The authors describe the field as entering a phase of targeted intervention, after years of primarily supportive care. (Based on articles retrieved from PubMed, PMID 42229424 — Cell Rep Med, doi:10.1016/j.xcrm.2026.102841)
