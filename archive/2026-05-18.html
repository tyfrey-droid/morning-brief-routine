🧬 Mito Research
  • A study published May 13 in Molecular Biology Reports found that blocking aerobic glycolysis with 2-deoxy-D-glucose (2-DG) significantly reduced seizure-induced neuronal death in rats by suppressing ferroptosis—a form of iron-dependent oxidative cell death—through activation of the AMPK–FoxO3a signaling pathway. While this is a rat model of general epilepsy rather than MELAS, ferroptosis driven by iron dysregulation and mitochondrial damage is an active research focus in MELAS pathology, and the AMPK pathway is a metabolic target of broad interest in mitochondrial disease; the findings suggest glycolysis inhibition as a neuroprotective strategy worth watching. (Molecular Biology Reports — PubMed PMID 42126735)
