✦ Faith & Theology

  • Pope Leo XIV issued Magnifica Humanitas ("Magnificent Humanity") on May 25 — his first papal encyclical and the first major papal text focused on artificial intelligence. At 42,300 words, it calls for the world to "disarm" AI by building stronger safeguards against its concentration of power and its capacity to displace workers and erode human agency. Leo deliberately chose his papal name to echo Leo XIII, whose 1891 Rerum Novarum addressed the upheavals of the Industrial Revolution. He argues technology is neither inherently evil nor neutral — it demands active moral governance. (Time, Vatican News, USCCB)
  • James Robison, televangelist and founder of Life Outreach International, died May 16 at age 82. Known as the "avenging angel of the religious right" in the 1980s, Robison was a prominent voice in evangelical politics from the Reagan era onward, and had in later years turned significant attention toward international humanitarian work — water access, child welfare, and food relief across Africa and Latin America. (Religion News Service)

🧬 Mito Research

  • Researchers at the San Raffaele Telethon Institute for Gene Therapy (Milan) demonstrated that a single systemic dose of a lentiviral vector carrying the MMUT gene produced durable correction of methylmalonic acidemia (MMA) in mice, with metabolic normalization lasting more than one year and no evidence of vector-related toxicity. MMA is a severe inherited mitochondrial enzyme deficiency distinct from MELAS; this study provides preclinical proof-of-concept that lentiviral gene therapy can achieve stable, long-lasting rescue in inherited metabolic disease affecting mitochondrial function in pediatric patients. Published May 25, 2026 in the Journal of Hepatology. According to PubMed (doi: 10.1016/j.jhep.2026.05.011, PMID 42191028)
  • A review published May 24 in Nucleosides, Nucleotides & Nucleic Acids surveys the current toolkit for gene therapy, RNA-based therapeutics, and CRISPR genome editing in neurodegenerative diseases involving mitochondrial dysfunction. The authors cover AAV vector delivery to the CNS, antisense oligonucleotides, siRNA/miRNA, synthetic mRNA, and CRISPR-based approaches, noting that dual-vector systems and capsid engineering are expanding AAV's capacity beyond its traditional ~4.7 kb limit. Most platforms remain at preclinical or early clinical stages. Broadly relevant to neuroprotection research in conditions with mitochondrial pathology. According to PubMed (doi: 10.1080/15257770.2026.2662373, PMID 42178743)
