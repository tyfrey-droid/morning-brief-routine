✨ One Good Thing
  • Calming the brain's immune cells restored sleep — A University of Kentucky study in Alzheimer's & Dementia found that overactive microglia — the brain's own immune cells — not amyloid plaques, drive the severe sleep loss that afflicts many people with Alzheimer's. When researchers used a drug to temporarily silence about 87% of those cells, mice with Alzheimer's pathology regained more than two hours of restorative sleep a night, without clearing a single plaque — pointing to a fresh target for one of the disease's most debilitating, caregiver-crushing symptoms. (University of Kentucky)
