🧬 Mito Research
  • A case report published May 1 in Medicine (Baltimore) described twin brothers carrying the m.3243A>G MELAS mutation who developed markedly different symptoms despite identical genetics — one with seizures and stroke-like episodes beginning at age 10, the other with hearing loss followed by epilepsy at 12. The case illustrates MELAS's significant phenotypic variability, which frequently delays diagnosis. Their mother was an asymptomatic carrier at 30.79% heteroplasmy, a reminder of how widely expression can diverge within the same family. Early genetic testing confirmed the mutation in both. (PubMed · DOI · Wu et al., Medicine, 2026)
  • Researchers at Seoul National University published results May 13 in Scientific Reports showing that PN-101 — a mitochondrial transplantation therapy using mitochondria derived from umbilical cord mesenchymal stem cells — significantly improved ATP production, Complex I activity, and OXPHOS protein levels in cells carrying a heteroplasmic mtDNA mutation (m.1555A>G, associated with hearing loss). Critically, repeated PN-101 treatments induced a sustained shift in heteroplasmy toward wild-type mtDNA. While the study targets a different mutation than MELAS, the heteroplasmy-shifting mechanism via mitochondrial transplantation may have broad relevance to mtDNA disorders. ⚠️ Industry-funded (Paean Biotechnology, Inc., Seoul). (PubMed · DOI · Kim et al., Scientific Reports, 2026)
