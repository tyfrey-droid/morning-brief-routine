🧬 Mito Research
  • A systematic review and meta-analysis published June 10 in Orphanet Journal of Rare Diseases pooled data from 988 MELAS patients across 13 studies, confirming stroke-like episodes in 99%, seizures in 89%, and elevated lactate in 81%, with the temporal lobe most frequently affected on imaging. Researchers identified four potential therapeutic targets — MNRR1, L-arginine, the PI3K-AKT-mTOR pathway, and the electron transport chain — as the most promising framework for multi-targeted treatment strategies currently lacking for this condition. (PubMed, DOI 10.1186/s13023-026-04448-6)
  • A comprehensive review published June 10 in Cellular and Molecular Life Sciences maps the current landscape of mitochondrial Complex I deficiency — the primary driver of MELAS, Leigh syndrome, and LHON — and surveys gene therapy, iPSC-based models, and metabolic modulator approaches under active investigation. The authors note no cures exist and translational challenges remain significant, but that the experimental model pipeline is meaningfully expanding, which is essential groundwork for developing effective interventions. (PubMed, DOI 10.1007/s00018-026-06169-2)
