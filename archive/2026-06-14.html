🧬  Mito Research
  • A systematic review and meta-analysis covering 988 MELAS patients — published June 10 in Orphanet Journal of Rare Diseases — found the m.3243A>G mutation in 78% of cases. The most prevalent symptoms were stroke-like episodes (99%), seizures (89%), and elevated lactate (81%), with the temporal lobe most commonly affected on imaging. Pathway analysis identified L-arginine, MNRR1, the PI3K-AKT-mTOR pathway, and the electron transport chain as the most promising targets for multi-targeted therapeutic development. (PubMed; Orphanet J Rare Dis — doi:10.1186/s13023-026-04448-6)
  • A comprehensive review published June 10 in Cellular and Molecular Life Sciences examines mitochondrial Complex I deficiency — the primary enzymatic failure underlying MELAS — and concludes that existing animal and cellular disease models cannot adequately capture the heteroplasmy variation and tissue-specific effects that define real patient experience. The authors call urgently for better human-relevant models (iPSC lines, organoids) to accelerate therapeutic development. Honest context: no cure currently exists. (PubMed; Cell Mol Life Sci — doi:10.1007/s00018-026-06169-2)
