🇺🇸 US & Politics
  • Trump hits Canada with 50% tariffs — President Trump signed proclamations imposing 50% tariffs on roughly $20 billion of Canadian goods — wine, dairy, cars, furniture, cement and more — invoking a dormant 1930 Tariff Act provision unused since 1949, in retaliation for provincial bans on US alcohol and Canadian dairy and auto rules. The duties, which apply even to USMCA-covered goods (energy and potash exempt), take effect in about 30 days; PM Mark Carney said Canada is open to talks but warned of higher US prices. (NPR, Al Jazeera, The Hill)
  • Disaster aid slowest on record — An Associated Press analysis of federal data going back to 1989 found President Trump has taken longer than any president in that span to approve major disaster declarations — about six weeks on average — and has denied a higher share of requests, approving roughly 80% from Republican governors versus about 60% from Democratic ones. The delays leave storm and flood victims waiting on federal help. (PBS NewsHour)

It frames the episode as evidence that watching individual actions isn't enough for persistent AI agents. (OpenAI, Neowin)
