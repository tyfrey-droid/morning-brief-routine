🧬 Mito Research
  • Cambridge University researchers published a study in Nature Communications (June 21) using human iPSC-derived cerebral organoids carrying the m.3243A>G mutation to trace how heteroplasmy level drives neurological damage. Organoids with high heteroplasmy — comparable to Emi's approximately 80% — showed preferential impairment of deep-layer cortical projection neurons, axonal degeneration, and apoptosis, closely matching brain autopsy findings from a MELAS patient. The organoid platform provides a first-of-its-kind human model for testing therapies targeting this specific mutation. (According to PubMed: Hathazi et al., Nat Commun, doi:10.1038/s41467-026-74103-y)
  • A comprehensive review published June 26 in Biomedicine & Pharmacotherapy surveyed 159 primary mitochondrial disease clinical trials from 2010–2026 and identified an emerging turning point. Four therapies now have regulatory authorization, including taurine specifically for MELAS. The review highlights heteroplasmy-targeting approaches, NAD⁺ augmentation, and gene therapies as the most active emerging strategies — with roughly half of all PMD interventional trials opened since 2020 and 50 studies currently recruiting. (According to PubMed: Wang & Jhuang, Biomed Pharmacother, doi:10.1016/j.biopha.2026.119699)
