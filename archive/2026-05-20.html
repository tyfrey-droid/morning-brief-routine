🧬 Mito Research
  • A peer-reviewed study published May 13 in Scientific Reports (Kim et al., Seoul National University / Paean Biotechnology) demonstrated that mitochondrial transplantation using donor mitochondria derived from umbilical cord mesenchymal stem cells induced a heteroplasmy shift toward wild-type mtDNA in patient-derived fibroblasts. The cells carried the m.1555A>G mutation — a different point mutation than Emi's m.3243A>G — but the underlying mechanism being tested is the same: introducing healthy donor mitochondria reduced the mutant-to-wild-type ratio. Repeated treatments sustained and enhanced the effect. The study also showed increased ATP production, improved Complex I activity, and protection against induced mitochondrial damage. What this means for the broader field: additional cell-culture evidence that mitochondrial transplantation can shift heteroplasmy, not merely supplement function around a fixed mutation burden. It does not address MELAS, stroke-like episodes, or neuronal tissue specifically; the gap between fibroblast results and neurons remains a major open question. ⚠️ Industry-funded: the majority of authors are Paean Biotechnology employees, and PN-101 is their commercial product. (Scientific Reports (PubMed), DOI: 10.1038/s41598-026-51402-4)
