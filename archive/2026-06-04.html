🌊 San Diego
  • San Diego voters rejected Measure A — the proposed vacancy tax on non-primary homes — by 58% to 42% in Tuesday's primary. The measure would have taxed homes left unoccupied for most of the year, starting at $8,000 annually in 2027 and rising to $10,000 in 2028; the city estimated it would raise $9–21 million per year. Opponents, funded primarily by the California Association of Realtors at $814,765, outspent supporters more than 7-to-1. (KPBS, inewsource)
