🌍 World
  • President Trump declared the US-Iran ceasefire "over" while at the NATO summit in Ankara, after US Central Command struck more than 80 Iranian targets and reimposed oil sanctions following Iranian attacks on three commercial vessels in the Strait of Hormuz; Iran's Revolutionary Guard said it retaliated by striking US military targets in Bahrain and Kuwait, though Trump said negotiators could keep talking. (Washington Post, Al Jazeera)

(NPR, The Hill)

(CNN, PBS)
  • The WHO says Ebola has killed more than 500 people among 1,561 confirmed cases in eastern Democratic Republic of Congo, calling it one of the deadliest starts to any Ebola epidemic on record; officials say the outbreak's true scale is still unclear, and a trial of two experimental treatments began July 2. (UN News, Al Jazeera)
  • The UN has warned that up to 500,000 civilians could be trapped as Sudan's Rapid Support Forces besiege the city of el-Obeid, with more than 11,000 people fleeing the surrounding North Kordofan region in the past two weeks amid near-daily drone strikes on the city's power and water infrastructure. (Al Jazeera, UN News)
  • A Paris appeals court convicted Marine Le Pen of embezzling European Parliament funds and ordered her to wear an electronic ankle monitor for a year, but reduced her office ban to a largely suspended term, clearing her to run in France's 2027 presidential election; Le Pen said she will appeal to France's highest court while continuing her campaign. (CNN, Washington Post)
  • Nigeria's government said two of its citizens were killed amid weeks of anti-migrant unrest in South Africa and warned conditions for African migrants there are deteriorating, announcing further evacuation flights; South African police disputed that one of the deaths was linked to the protests. (Al Jazeera, Washington Post)
  • Record monsoon rainfall has killed at least 13 people in Mumbai and its suburbs over the past several days, including six in a building collapse, as local trains were disrupted and flights cancelled; India's weather service has issued four consecutive "red alert" warnings, its highest severity level. (Bloomberg, US News)

(CNN, Bloomberg)

(Washington Post, NBC News)
  • Pressure on Maine Senate candidate Graham Platner intensified as New York Mayor Zohran Mamdani's top political adviser traveled to Maine to help wind down Platner's campaign, and the Maine Democratic Party said he will have "no role" in choosing a replacement if he withdraws; Republicans are simultaneously preparing an $8 million ad campaign against whoever replaces him, with Platner's withdrawal deadline set for 5 p.m. Monday, July 13. (Axios, Portland Press Herald)
  • Supreme Court Justices Elena Kagan and Amy Coney Barrett are scheduled to testify before House and Senate Appropriations panels on July 14 about the judiciary's fiscal 2027 budget request, which seeks $9.7 billion including increased funding for Supreme Court Police and courthouse security amid a rise in threats against federal judges — the first time justices have testified before Congress since 2019. (Bloomberg, The Hill)
  • A lawsuit filed in Washington, DC alleges that US immigration agencies have shared confidential asylum-application details of Iranian asylum seekers with Iran's Interests Section during monthly meetings since March 2025, potentially endangering pro-democracy activists, religious minorities and LGBTQ applicants; ICE denies the allegations, and the suit — brought by Public Citizen on behalf of the Iranian American Legal Defense Fund — seeks an injunction and an independent monitor. (NPR, Washington Post)

(CBS News, The Hill)
