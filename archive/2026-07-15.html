🌍 World
  • Trump reverses Hormuz "toll" demand after Gulf backlash. President Trump abandoned his push to charge a 20% fee on cargo transiting the Strait of Hormuz one day after announcing it, following objections from Gulf leaders and a UN maritime agency warning that the toll violated international law; he said the U.S. will pursue trade and investment deals with Gulf states instead. Opposition parties have already petitioned the High Court to block the law. (Times of Israel, Haaretz)
  • US to fully exit Iraq by September 30. Iraqi Prime Minister Ali al-Zaidi said after meeting Trump at the White House that all U.S. forces will leave Iraq by Sept. 30, ending the 23-year military presence that began with the 2003 invasion; roughly 2,000-2,500 advisory and counter-ISIS personnel will depart under the existing 2024 drawdown agreement. (Al-Monitor, Washington Times)
  • June deadliest month for Ukrainian civilians since 2022. The UN's human rights monitoring mission says at least 293 Ukrainian civilians were killed and nearly 1,990 wounded in June, the worst monthly toll since April 2022, driven by intensified long-range Russian missile strikes on Kyiv, Dnipro and Odesa; year-to-date civilian deaths are up 37% over the same period last year. (CNN, France 24)
  • Sudan sentences RSF's Hemedti to death in absentia. A Port Sudan court sentenced Rapid Support Forces commander Mohamed Hamdan Dagalo, known as Hemedti, and 15 other RSF figures to death for war crimes and genocide tied to the 2023 killing of West Darfur's governor — the first court verdict against Hemedti since Sudan's civil war began in 2023. (Al Jazeera, Middle East Monitor)
  • China's growth slows to 4.3% amid oil shock. China's economy grew 4.3% year-on-year in the second quarter, below forecasts and down from 5.0% in Q1 — the slowest pace since late 2022 — as an 18% slump in property investment and Iran-war-driven oil price effects offset a 27% surge in exports. (Bloomberg, NBC News)
