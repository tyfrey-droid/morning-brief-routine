🧬 Mito Research
  • A systematic review and meta-analysis of MELAS — published June 10 in Orphanet Journal of Rare Diseases (Li et al., Xi'an Jiaotong-Liverpool University / University of Liverpool) — pooled data from 988 patients across 13 studies. The m.3243A>G mutation was confirmed in 78% of cases. The most common manifestations: stroke-like episodes (99%), seizures (89%), elevated lactate (81%). Temporal lobe involvement was most frequent on imaging. Pathway analysis identified MNRR1 signaling, L-arginine metabolism, the PI3K-AKT-mTOR pathway, and the mitochondrial electron transport chain as the most promising multi-targeted therapeutic directions — a useful framework for where future drug development may focus. (PubMed, doi:10.1186/s13023-026-04448-6)
  • A phase II open-label trial at McGill University Health Centre, published June 15 in Neurotherapeutics (Cheung et al.), followed 25 children and young adults (mean age 12.3) with POLG-related mitochondrial disorders. Treatment with a deoxycytidine/deoxythymidine combination improved Newcastle Mitochondrial Disease Scale scores at every timepoint from 1 to 24 months; quality of life also improved at 3, 12, and 18 months. Not MELAS specifically, but within the same mitochondrial disease family — the deoxynucleoside strategy is being watched for potential broader application. (PubMed, doi:10.1016/j.neurot.2026.e00945)
