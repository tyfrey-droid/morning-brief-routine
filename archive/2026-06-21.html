🧬  Mito Research
  • A systematic review and meta-analysis of 988 MELAS patients — published June 10 in Orphanet Journal of Rare Diseases — confirms the m.3243A>G mutation in 78% of cases and documents stroke-like episodes in 99%, seizures in 89%, and elevated lactate in 81%. Pathway analysis identifies L-arginine, the PI3K-AKT-mTOR pathway, and the electron transport chain as the most promising multi-targeted therapeutic targets in MELAS, providing a framework for future treatment development. (Orphanet J. Rare Dis. — PubMed 42271506)
  • A clinical commentary published June 18 in Revista Española de Enfermedades Digestivas discusses an 18-year-old patient with the m.3243A>G mutation presenting with both MELAS syndrome and severe chronic intestinal pseudo-obstruction (CIPO) — a gastrointestinal complication seen in a subset of mitochondrial disease patients.   • A comprehensive review of mitochondrial complex I deficiency-associated diseases — including MELAS — published June 10 in Cellular and Molecular Life Sciences calls for urgent development of human-relevant disease models. Current preclinical models (mouse models, patient-derived fibroblasts, cybrids, iPSCs) are limited by variable heteroplasmy and tissue-specific effects, leaving basic disease mechanisms poorly understood. Gene therapy and targeted molecular interventions are flagged as the most promising avenues requiring better translational pathways. (Cell Mol Life Sci. — PubMed 42265384)
