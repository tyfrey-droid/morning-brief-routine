🌍 World
  • The WHO declared a Public Health Emergency of International Concern (PHEIC) on May 17 as the Bundibugyo strain of Ebola spreads through conflict-torn Ituri province in the DRC and into Uganda. As of May 29, at least 1,262 suspected and confirmed cases and 241 deaths have been recorded, with a case fatality rate of 30–50%. No vaccine or targeted treatment exists for this Ebola strain. Director-General Tedros traveled to Ituri this week, warning that ongoing insecurity and attacks on health facilities are making contact-tracing “nearly impossible.” (Al Jazeera, WHO, UN News)

A drone attack destroyed the passenger railway station in Shostka, Sumy Oblast; attacks killed 5 and injured 40 across the country. Ukraine’s forces struck two Russian Tu-142 naval patrol aircraft and an Iskander missile system at the Taganrog airfield in Rostov Oblast, and the village of Novoselivka in Dnipropetrovsk Oblast was reported liberated. (empr.media, Kyiv Independent)
