🇺🇸 US & Politics
  • The Supreme Court ruled 6-3 on June 30 that the Constitution guarantees automatic birthright citizenship to children born on U.S. soil, striking down President Trump's day-one executive order that sought to deny citizenship to babies born to parents who are undocumented or on temporary visas. Chief Justice John Roberts wrote the majority opinion; Justice Clarence Thomas wrote a 91-page dissent. (NPR, NBC News)
  • In a separate 6-3 ruling on June 29 (Trump v. Slaughter), the Supreme Court overturned the 90-year-old Humphrey's Executor precedent, holding that presidents may fire commissioners of independent agencies like the FTC at will. The decision upheld Trump's 2025 removal of FTC Commissioner Rebecca Slaughter and is expected to extend presidential control over other independent bodies. (NPR, CBS News)
  • The Supreme Court also struck down a 1974 law limiting how much national political parties can spend in coordination with their candidates, ruling 6-3 that the restriction violated the First Amendment. Justice Brett Kavanaugh wrote the majority opinion; the ruling is expected to increase party-coordinated spending ahead of the November midterms. (NPR, NBC News)
  • In a fourth end-of-term ruling on June 30, the court found 6-3 that states may ban transgender girls from competing on girls' and women's school sports teams, rejecting challenges to bans in West Virginia and Idaho. Justice Kavanaugh wrote for the majority; the decision is expected to affect roughly 25 states with similar laws. (NPR, CBS News)
