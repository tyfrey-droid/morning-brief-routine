🌍 World
  • The fragile US–Iran ceasefire frayed further on June 6 when Iran launched four one-way attack drones toward the Strait of Hormuz. CENTCOM shot them down, then struck Iranian coastal radar sites on Qeshm Island and at Goruk in response. Tehran called the strikes a "flagrant violation" of the truce. Ship traffic through the strait has been at a near-standstill since Iran declared it "closed" in March, disrupting global oil, LNG, and fertilizer supply chains. (RFE/RL, CNN, Times of Israel)
  • Pope Leo XIV is presiding over a Corpus Christi mass and procession in Madrid's Plaza de Cibeles today, drawing 1.2 million people. It is the first papal visit to Spain in 15 years. His six-day itinerary — through Madrid, Barcelona, and the Canary Islands — emphasizes ministry to migrants and the poor. He will become the first pope to address the Spanish Parliament in a joint session on June 8, and will inaugurate the Sagrada Familia's final tower on June 10. (Vatican News, Time)
  • Armenians are voting today in their first regularly scheduled parliamentary election since 2017. Incumbent PM Nikol Pashinyan faces businessman Samvel Karapetyan in a contest widely read as a choice between continued Western orientation and return to Russia's orbit — freighted by the 2023 loss of Nagorno-Karabakh and the ongoing regional fallout from the Iran war. (EVN Report, Christian Science Monitor)
  • Iran's World Cup squad relocated its training base from Arizona to Tijuana after weeks of visa disputes with Washington. All 26 players ultimately received US entry visas, though several staff members were denied. It is the first World Cup since 1930 in which a host nation will face a country it is actively at war with. (Al Jazeera)
