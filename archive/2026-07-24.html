🌍 World
  • Wildfires force mass evacuations in Europe — Spain declared a national emergency Friday and France appealed to the EU for help as heatwave-driven wildfires burned across Spain, France and Italy. More than 60,000 people have left parts of southwestern France, including the Cap Ferret peninsula, and thousands more around Madrid, where officials call the fires the region's worst on record; three firefighters — two in France, one in Italy — have died. (NBC News, France 24, Euronews)
  • Gaza famine eases, monitors caution — The IPC, the global authority on food-insecurity classification, said the famine conditions it declared last year have eased after reduced fighting allowed more aid into Gaza, downgrading all five governorates to Phase 3 "crisis." Monitors stressed the gains are fragile, with more than 200,000 people still in Phase 4 "emergency" and roughly 1.4 million facing acute food insecurity through year's end. (Washington Post, US News)
🇺🇸 US & Politics
  • New forced-labor tariffs hit 60 nations — After the Supreme Court struck down his blanket "reciprocal" tariffs, President Trump imposed fresh 10%–12.5% duties on 60 trade partners — covering about 99% of US imports — just after midnight Friday, citing weak enforcement of bans on forced-labor goods. The levies rely on Section 301 of the 1974 Trade Act rather than the emergency-powers law the court rejected; oil, gas and fertilizer are exempt. (NBC News, CNBC, NPR)
  • DOJ withdraws New York Times subpoenas — The Justice Department dropped subpoenas seeking grand-jury testimony and phone records from Times reporters who covered security concerns over Trump's Qatari-donated Air Force One, a day after a federal judge sharply questioned the government over "profound First Amendment" issues. Judge Arun Subramanian said investigators should have exhausted other sources before targeting journalists. (NPR, CNBC, Axios)

(Apple, TechCrunch, 9to5Mac)

The company framed it as a boundary case in adversarial testing; outside researchers called it a warning about how much autonomy AI agents should be given. (Al Jazeera, Washington Post)

(Samsung Newsroom, Engadget)
