✦  Faith & Theology
  • Pope Leo XIV's Magnifica Humanitas — released today — is the first papal document to directly address artificial intelligence, and it arrives as a landmark in Catholic social teaching. Leo grounds the encyclical in the long tradition of the Church speaking at moments of industrial upheaval, invoking his namesake Leo XIII's 1891 labor encyclical Rerum Novarum. The document argues that AI must not serve the powerful few, warns of its role fueling global warfare, and calls the Church to bear witness to human dignity in a technological age. Religion News Service notes its framing as fundamentally ecclesial: the document is not a policy paper but a theological call. (Religion News Service, National Catholic Reporter, America Magazine)

🧬  Mito Research
  • A study published May 13 in Scientific Reports shows that mitochondrial transplantation — introducing healthy donor mitochondria derived from umbilical cord mesenchymal stem cells — can shift heteroplasmy toward wild-type mtDNA in patient cells and that repeated treatments sustained and extended this shift. The work used cells carrying the m.1555A>G mutation (linked to hearing loss, not MELAS directly), but the central finding — that external mitochondrial transfer can reduce the proportion of mutant mtDNA — is mechanistically relevant to heteroplasmy reduction strategies for m.3243A>G MELAS. Researchers from Seoul National University Bundang Hospital and Paean Biotechnology conducted the study. (⚠️ industry-funded: Paean Biotechnology developed PN-101, the transplantation therapy tested) (Scientific Reports via PubMed — PMID 42115265)
