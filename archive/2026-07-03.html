🌍 World
  • Iran's dayslong state funeral for Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei began in Tehran Friday, with representatives from more than 100 countries and an estimated 15-20 million mourners expected over the coming week; indirect US-Iran talks, which made "positive progress" in this week's Doha round, are now formally paused until after the funeral concludes July 9. (Euronews, Al Jazeera)

(Washington Post, Al Jazeera)
  • The UN Human Rights Council held an urgent debate in Geneva Friday over the RSF's siege of El Obeid, Sudan, considering a draft resolution that would direct the UN's independent fact-finding mission to investigate alleged war crimes and the use of starvation as a weapon; roughly 500,000 civilians are estimated to be at risk, with a council vote expected by Tuesday. (OHCHR, Al Jazeera)
  • Afghanistan's Taliban government said it launched retaliatory airstrikes into Pakistan's Balochistan province targeting an alleged ISIS-K camp; Islamabad said it intercepted four drones. The exchange follows Pakistani strikes earlier in the week that killed at least 28 civilians in Afghanistan, part of a border conflict that has killed hundreds this year. (US News, Al Jazeera)

(CBS News, ABC News)

(CBS News, Roll Call)
