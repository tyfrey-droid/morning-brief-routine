🧬 Mito Research
  • Based on articles retrieved from PubMed: A case report published May 1, 2026 in Medicine (Baltimore) documents twin brothers with MELAS who both carry the m.3243A>G mutation, yet presented very differently — the elder developing seizures, blurred vision, and exercise intolerance from age 10; the younger experiencing hearing loss first, then persistent epilepsy at age 12. While a case report rather than a treatment breakthrough, it adds to the evidence base for understanding the condition's variable presentation — including why two people with the same mutation can experience it so differently.
