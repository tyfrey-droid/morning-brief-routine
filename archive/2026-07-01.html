🧬 Mito Research
  • A new study in Nature Communications from University of Cambridge researchers created the first human brain model of the m.3243A>G mutation using lab-grown cerebral organoids, and found that high mitochondrial heteroplasmy selectively damages "deep-layer" cortical neurons (long-range projection neurons), causing axonal degeneration and cell death that mirrors what was seen in an actual MELAS patient's brain autopsy tissue. It's the first model of its kind for this exact mutation and helps explain which brain circuits are most vulnerable at high heteroplasmy levels (~80%, matching Emi's profile) — potentially guiding future targeted therapies, though it is not itself a treatment. (Nature Communications)
