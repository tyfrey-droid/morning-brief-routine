🧬 Mito Research
  • A phase 1b open-label trial of mavodelpar — a selective PPAR-delta agonist that targets oxidative phosphorylation, the same energy pathway disrupted in MELAS — published its results this week in Scientific Reports. Among 23 adults with primary mitochondrial myopathy (the disease class that includes MELAS), 17 completed the 12-week treatment phase; participants showed a mean improvement of 104 meters on the twelve-minute walk test (95% CI: 53–156m), with mostly mild-to-moderate adverse effects (headache and constipation, 17% each). The extension phase was cut short during the COVID-19 pandemic before full enrollment was completed — a significant limitation. The underlying trial was conducted circa 2020–2022; these are its first published peer-reviewed results. The signal is modest but real, and PPAR-delta agonism remains an active investigational direction for mitochondrial disease. (PubMed PMID 42151205 / Scientific Reports, published May 18, 2026)
