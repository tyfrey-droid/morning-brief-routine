  • Bertha comes ashore in Louisiana — Tropical Storm Bertha made landfall in St. Bernard Parish, La., around 2 p.m. CT Wednesday — the first landfalling storm of the 2026 Atlantic season — with peak winds near 60 mph and 2–3 feet of coastal inundation from Pensacola westward. Damage appeared limited, and forecasters expect the weakening system to reach the Texas coast Thursday before dissipating Friday. (NPR, CBS News)
  • US and Saudis sign a civil nuclear deal — Energy Secretary Chris Wright and Saudi Energy Minister Prince Abdulaziz bin Salman signed a "123 agreement" establishing the legal framework for American companies to build and fuel a Saudi civilian nuclear program, alongside a separate nuclear-safeguards pact. The Energy Department called it the basis of a decades-long partnership worth billions; it now goes to Congress, where some lawmakers worry Riyadh could later pursue weapons. (CNBC, NBC News)
