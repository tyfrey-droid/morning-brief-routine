🧬 Mito Research
  • A case report published May 1 in Medicine (Baltimore) documents identical twin brothers both carrying the m.3243A>G MELAS mutation — yet presenting with strikingly different clinical profiles. One twin's first symptoms were seizures and learning difficulties at age 10; the other's were hearing loss at 12 followed by epilepsy. Their mother was confirmed as an asymptomatic carrier at ~31% heteroplasmy. The case reinforces the significant phenotypic variability of MELAS even within identical genetics, and highlights the importance of early genetic confirmation and tailored treatment — including levetiracetam, CoQ10, arginine, and levocarnitine. (Based on articles retrieved from PubMed: Medicine (Baltimore), DOI 10.1097/MD.0000000000047264, PMID 42065208)
  • A study published May 13 in Scientific Reports from Paean Biotechnology (Seoul) demonstrates that mitochondrial transplantation using PN-101 — mitochondria derived from umbilical cord mesenchymal stem cells — can shift cellular heteroplasmy toward wild-type mtDNA in patient fibroblasts carrying an mtDNA point mutation (m.1555A>G, a hearing-loss variant). Critically, repeated transplantation sessions sustained and amplified the heteroplasmy shift over time. While the target mutation differs from Emi's m.3243A>G, the demonstration that externally delivered mitochondria can durably move the mutant/wild-type ratio is a meaningful proof of concept for heteroplasmy reduction in mtDNA diseases broadly. ⚠️ industry-funded (Paean Biotechnology). (Based on articles retrieved from PubMed: Scientific Reports, DOI 10.1038/s41598-026-51402-4, PMID 42115265)
