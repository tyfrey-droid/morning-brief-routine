🌍 World
  • A 7.2-magnitude foreshock struck near Caracas on June 24, followed less than a minute later by a 7.5-magnitude quake — Venezuela’s most powerful seismic event in over a century. The confirmed death toll has reached at least 235, with the U.S. Geological Survey estimating a 44% chance the final count exceeds 10,000. Urban search-and-rescue teams from Fairfax County (VA) and Los Angeles County have deployed alongside responders from Mexico, Brazil, Colombia, Cuba, and Canada. A state of emergency remains in effect as rescue workers continue to search collapsed buildings. (NPR, Al Jazeera, NBC News)
  • The Ebola outbreak in eastern Democratic Republic of Congo has surpassed 1,118 confirmed cases and 291 deaths as of June 24, making it the second-largest Ebola outbreak on record — and one rising faster than any previous outbreak. The disease, caused by the Bundibugyo virus strain, has crossed into Uganda; France reported one imported confirmed case on June 24. WHO, MSF, and UN agencies are scaling up response across affected provinces in Ituri, North Kivu, and South Kivu. (WHO, Doctors Without Borders, UN News, CDC)

(SCOTUSblog, NPR)
