🧬 Mito Research
  • According to PubMed, a systematic review and meta-analysis of 988 MELAS patients—published June 10 in Orphanet Journal of Rare Diseases (Li et al., Xi'an Jiaotong-Liverpool University / University of Liverpool)—confirmed the m.3243A>G mutation in 78% of cases. The most common features were stroke-like episodes (99%), seizures (89%), and elevated lactate (81%), with the temporal lobe most frequently affected on imaging (87%). Pathway analysis identified mitophagy/autophagy, ROS generation, and energy metabolism as the three core disease mechanisms, flagging L-arginine, MNRR1, and the PI3K-AKT-mTOR pathway as the most promising multi-targeted therapeutic candidates. (PubMed · DOI: 10.1186/s13023-026-04448-6)
  • According to PubMed, a comprehensive review of mitochondrial complex I deficiency—published June 10 in Cellular and Molecular Life Sciences (Jentsch & Ventura, Heinrich Heine University Düsseldorf)—covers MELAS, Leigh syndrome, and LHON as the most common complex I diseases and finds that no cures exist and only limited symptomatic therapies are available. The authors identify patient-derived fibroblasts, cybrid cells, and iPSC-derived neurons as the primary tools currently enabling therapeutic research, and issue an urgent call for better human-relevant disease models to close the translational gap from lab to clinic. (PubMed · DOI: 10.1007/s00018-026-06169-2)
