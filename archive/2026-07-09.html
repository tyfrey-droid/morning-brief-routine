🧬 Mito Research
  • According to PubMed, a University of Toronto/SickKids study published June 27 in Mitochondrion found that knocking down the mitochondrial fission protein DNM1L accelerates heteroplasmy shifting — the process of a cell's mitochondria population moving away from a harmful mutation — cutting the mutant load of a Leigh-syndrome-causing mtDNA variant (m.10191C) by 10% in just 3 weeks, versus 5 weeks using the drug berberine alone; the effect runs through an autophagy pathway involving the protein ATG7. It targets a different mutation than Emi's m.3243A>G, but it adds a new, mechanistically distinct lever to the small and growing toolkit for heteroplasmy reduction — the strategy researchers increasingly see as key to future MELAS treatment. (Mitochondrion — PubMed)
