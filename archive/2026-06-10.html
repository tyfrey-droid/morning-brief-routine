🧬  Mito Research
  • According to PubMed, a review published June 2 in Cell Reports Medicine by researchers at UCL Queen Square Institute of Neurology — including specialists from the NHS Highly Specialised Service for Rare Mitochondrial Disorders — surveys the current landscape of emerging therapies for mtDNA-related diseases including MELAS. The paper covers allotopic expression, mitochondria-targeted nucleases, next-generation base editors (which can reduce pathogenic mtDNA variants such as m.3243A>G), pharmacological heteroplasmy modulation, mitochondrial donation, and mitochondrial transplantation. The authors describe progress as real but note that disease-modifying therapies "remain limited" — a frank assessment from a leading clinical center. (Shi et al., Cell Reports Medicine — DOI: 10.1016/j.xcrm.2026.102841)
  • According to PubMed, a review published today in Cellular and Molecular Life Sciences from Heinrich Heine University Düsseldorf examines the genetic basis and experimental models of mitochondrial Complex I deficiency diseases — the category encompassing MELAS, Leigh syndrome, and LHON. The authors identify heteroplasmy variation and tissue-specific effects as key barriers to translating research into effective therapies, and call urgently for better human-relevant in vivo and in vitro models. The paper maps where the field is actively investing and where the bottlenecks remain. (Jentsch & Ventura, Cell Mol Life Sci — DOI: 10.1007/s00018-026-06169-2)
