🧬  Mito Research
  • A systematic review and meta-analysis published June 10 in Orphanet Journal of Rare Diseases pooled data from 988 MELAS patients across 13 studies and confirmed the m.3243A>G mutation in 78% of cases, with stroke-like episodes in 99%, seizures in 89%, and elevated lactate in 81% — and identified the temporal lobe as the most affected brain region. The authors used pathway analysis to flag the most promising therapeutic targets: L-arginine supplementation, PI3K-AKT-mTOR signaling, the electron transport chain, and mitophagy/autophagy regulation — providing a framework for designing multi-targeted treatment strategies. (PubMed · doi:10.1186/s13023-026-04448-6)
  • A review published June 10 in Cellular and Molecular Life Sciences surveys the genetic basis of mitochondrial complex I (CI) deficiency — the primary driver of MELAS, Leigh syndrome, and LHON — and assesses the current landscape of gene therapy strategies, metabolic modulators, antioxidants, and mitophagy activators. While preclinical models show promise, the authors highlight heteroplasmy variability and tissue-specific effects as the central barriers to clinical translation, and call for better human-relevant in vivo models. (PubMed · doi:10.1007/s00018-026-06169-2)
