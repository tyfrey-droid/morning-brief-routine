🧬 Mito Research

  • A systematic review and meta-analysis published June 10 in Orphanet Journal of Rare Diseases synthesized data from 988 MELAS patients across 13 studies. The m.3243A>G mutation was confirmed in 78% of cases. Most common manifestations: stroke-like episodes (99%), seizures (89%), elevated lactate (81%), with the temporal lobe most frequently affected on imaging. Pathway analysis identified L-arginine, the PI3K-AKT-mTOR pathway, and the electron transport chain as the most promising multi-target therapeutic entry points—a potential framework for combination therapies. (PubMed · doi:10.1186/s13023-026-04448-6)
  • A review published June 10 in Cellular and Molecular Life Sciences examined disease models for mitochondrial complex I deficiency—the mechanistic root of MELAS, Leigh syndrome, and LHON. The authors conclude existing mouse and cell models still fail to capture the heteroplasmy variation and tissue-specific effects that define these diseases in patients, and call urgently for human-relevant models to accelerate therapeutic development. (PubMed · doi:10.1007/s00018-026-06169-2)
