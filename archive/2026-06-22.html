  • According to PubMed, a systematic review and meta-analysis of MELAS published June 10 in Orphanet Journal of Rare Diseases (Li et al., Xi’an Jiaotong-Liverpool University / University of Liverpool) pooled data from 13 studies covering 988 patients and confirmed a 78% pooled prevalence of the m.3243A>G mutation.
