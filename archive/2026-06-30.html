🇺🇸 US & Politics
  • In a 6–3 ruling on June 29, the Supreme Court overturned Humphrey's Executor (1935), the 91-year-old precedent that shielded members of independent federal agencies from presidential removal without cause. The decision upholds Trump's dismissal of FTC commissioner Rebecca Kelly Slaughter and effectively ends the structural independence of more than two dozen regulatory bodies. A separate 5–4 holding gives Federal Reserve Board member Lisa Cook the right to written notice and response before any termination — the Fed's independence received partial, not full, protection. (NPR, Washington Post, NBC News)
  • The Supreme Court ruled 6–3 on June 25 to allow the Trump administration to strip Temporary Protected Status from approximately 350,000 Haitians and 6,000 Syrians, holding that federal law bars courts from reviewing TPS termination decisions. The majority also rejected a racial discrimination claim in the Haiti case — Justice Kagan's dissent quoted Trump's inflammatory remarks about Haiti in response. The Syria discrimination claim survives in lower courts. Over a million immigrants now face an uncertain scramble for alternate legal status. (NPR, SCOTUSblog, CNN)
  • Eight people convicted of terrorism charges for the July 4, 2025 attack on the Prairieland ICE detention facility in Alvarado, Texas were sentenced June 23 to between 30 and 100 years in federal prison.
