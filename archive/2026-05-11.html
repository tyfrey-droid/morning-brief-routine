🧬  Mito Research
  • A peer-reviewed case report published May 1 in Medicine (Baltimore) documents twin brothers who both carry the m.3243A>G MELAS mutation but developed dramatically different clinical courses — one with seizures, stroke-like episodes, and learning difficulties beginning at age 10; the other primarily with hearing loss and epilepsy beginning at 12. Their mother carries the same mutation at only 30.79% heteroplasmy and remains asymptomatic. Both were treated with levetiracetam, coenzyme Q10, arginine, and levocarnitine; imaging showed the characteristic migratory lesions that shifted with each episode. The case is a clear illustration of how widely outcomes can diverge even with identical mutations and similar heteroplasmy — and it reinforces that early genetic testing is essential for diagnosis rather than waiting for a full clinical picture to emerge. (According to PubMed: Medicine (Baltimore), doi:10.1097/MD.0000000000047264)
