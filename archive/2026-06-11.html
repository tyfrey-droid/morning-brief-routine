🧬   Mito Research
  • A systematic review and meta-analysis published June 10 in Orphanet Journal of Rare Diseases analyzed 988 MELAS patients across 13 studies, finding the m.3243A>G mutation present in 78% of cases. The most prevalent manifestations were stroke-like episodes (99%), seizures (89%), and elevated lactate (81%), with the temporal lobe most frequently affected on imaging. Pathway analysis identified MNRR1, L-arginine, the PI3K-AKT-mTOR cascade, and the electron transport chain as the most promising multi-target therapeutic nodes for future combination treatments. (Li et al., Orphanet J Rare Dis — PubMed)
  • Also published June 10 in Cellular and Molecular Life Sciences, a comprehensive review of mitochondrial complex I (CI) deficiency — the primary enzymatic driver of MELAS, Leigh syndrome, and LHON — found that no cures exist and that translational progress is hampered by disease models that fail to capture tissue-specific heteroplasmy effects. Authors at Heinrich Heine University Düsseldorf argue that better human-relevant in vivo and in vitro models are the critical bottleneck to effective therapy development. (Jentsch & Ventura, Cell Mol Life Sci — PubMed)
