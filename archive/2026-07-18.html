🧬 Mito Research
  • Phase III drug holds up in a mitochondrial disease — In a randomized, placebo-controlled Phase III trial published July 17, patients with Leigh syndrome — a primary mitochondrial disease with CNS involvement — who had first improved on SPP-004 (5-aminolevulinic acid plus sodium ferrous citrate) were far less likely to lose that benefit when kept on the drug than when switched to placebo: about 15% of the treated group discontinued for inadequate efficacy over 48 weeks versus 50% on placebo, with only mild side effects. It is not MELAS and not a cure, but it is a rare randomized signal that a metabolic therapy can maintain clinical response in a CNS-affecting mitochondrial disease, using the same Newcastle pediatric scale applied across mito disorders. According to PubMed; ⚠️ industry-funded (developer SBI Pharmaceuticals co-authored). (PLOS One)
