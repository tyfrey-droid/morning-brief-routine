🧬 Mito Research
  • A case report published May 1 in Medicine (Baltimore) describes twin brothers both carrying the m.3243A>G MELAS mutation who presented with markedly different symptoms — the elder with stroke-like episodes, seizures, and exercise intolerance; the younger with hearing loss followed by epilepsy. The case underscores MELAS's significant phenotypic variability even within identical genetic backgrounds, and reinforces the importance of early genetic testing and family screening. Treatment described included arginine, CoQ10, levetiracetam, and ATP disodium. (Wu H, Wang Y, Kong Q. PubMed · Medicine, May 2026)
  • Published today in Scientific Reports, a South Korean team demonstrated that mitochondrial transplantation using stem-cell–derived mitochondria (PN-101, from umbilical cord mesenchymal stem cells) can shift heteroplasmy from mutant toward wild-type mtDNA in patient cells — and that repeated treatments sustain and amplify this shift. The study used the m.1555A>G mutation (hearing loss), but the core mechanism — displacing pathogenic mtDNA via allogeneic mitochondria — is directly relevant to high-heteroplasmy conditions including MELAS. The authors describe it as "a promising disease-modifying therapy." (Kim Y et al. PubMed · Scientific Reports, May 13 2026)
