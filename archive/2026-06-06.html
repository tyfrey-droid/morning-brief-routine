🧬 Mito Research
  • Based on articles retrieved from PubMed: A comprehensive review of emerging therapeutic strategies for mitochondrial DNA-related diseases was published June 2 in Cell Reports Medicine by a team at UCL Queen Square Institute of Neurology and the NHS Highly Specialised Service for Rare Mitochondrial Disorders in London — one of the world's foremost clinical and research centers for mito disease. Authors include Michael G. Hanna and Robert Pitceathly, who lead the UK's national mito service. The review surveys the current state of mtDNA-targeted interventions, including: allotopic expression (delivering corrected gene copies via the nucleus to compensate for defective mitochondrial genes); mitochondria-targeted nucleases designed to selectively reduce mutant mtDNA copies; next-generation base editors that can directly correct pathogenic variants such as m.3243A>G; pharmacological approaches to shift heteroplasmy; mitochondrial donation (reproductive); and mitochondrial transplantation. The authors frame these approaches as increasingly precise and mechanistic rather than purely supportive — though no approved disease-modifying therapy yet exists. (Cell Reports Medicine — DOI: 10.1016/j.xcrm.2026.102841, PubMed PMID 42229424)
