🧬 Mito Research
  • A comprehensive review published today in Molecular Biomedicine surveys the therapeutic landscape for mitochondrial dysfunction in neurodegenerative and inherited mitochondrial disorders, cataloguing current approaches: gene-targeted therapies (including AAV-based delivery), organelle replacement strategies, metabolic modulators, antioxidants, and emerging epitranscriptomic interventions. The authors highlight shared pathogenic mechanisms — disrupted oxidative phosphorylation, mitochondrial dynamics failure, impaired quality control pathways — that may make certain treatment strategies broadly applicable across conditions including MELAS. (Based on articles retrieved from PubMed — Tong Y et al., Mol Biomed, May 28, 2026; DOI 10.1186/s43556-026-00480-x)
  • Researchers at San Raffaele Telethon Institute for Gene Therapy (Milan) published preclinical proof-of-concept for liver-directed lentiviral gene therapy in methylmalonic acidemia (MMA), a related inherited mitochondrial metabolic disorder. A single dose in pediatric mice produced durable metabolic normalization lasting more than one year — correcting liver, kidney, and brain dysfunction — with corrected hepatocytes gaining a selective proliferative advantage that may lower the required therapeutic dose. While MMA involves a nuclear-encoded mitochondrial enzyme rather than a mtDNA mutation, the demonstration of stable, long-lasting genomic correction in a young, growing liver is directly informative for the broader question of what gene therapy can achieve in childhood mitochondrial disease. (Based on articles retrieved from PubMed — Barbon E et al., J Hepatol, May 25, 2026; DOI 10.1016/j.jhep.2026.05.011)
