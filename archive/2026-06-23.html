🧪 Mito Research
  • A systematic review and meta-analysis published June 10 in Orphanet Journal of Rare Diseases pooled data from 988 MELAS patients across 13 studies. Key findings: m.3243A > G mutation confirmed in 78% of cases; stroke-like episodes present in 99%, seizures in 89%, elevated lactate in 81%. Pathway analysis identified L-arginine, the PI3K–AKT–mTOR axis, MNRR1, and the electron transport chain as the most promising multi-targeted therapeutic intervention points. (Orphanet J Rare Dis — PubMed PMID 42271506)
  • A June 18 commentary in Revista Española de Enfermedades Digestivas discusses a MELAS case involving m.3243A > G presenting with both classic encephalopathy and severe chronic intestinal pseudo-obstruction. Despite surgical and conservative interventions including mitochondrial therapy, the patient died of multiple organ failure — a sobering reminder of the systemic reach of high-heteroplasmy disease and the urgency for earlier intervention. (Rev Esp Enferm Dig — PubMed PMID 42313089)
  • A review published June 10 in Cellular and Molecular Life Sciences surveys current disease models for mitochondrial complex I deficiency — the enzyme pathway central to MELAS and Leigh syndrome — covering fibroblasts, cybrids, iPSC-derived neurons, and mouse knockouts. The authors call for more human-relevant in vivo models to support therapeutic development. (Cell Mol Life Sci — PubMed PMID 42265384)
