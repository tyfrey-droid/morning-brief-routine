🧬  Mito Research
  • A case report published May 1, 2026 in Medicine (Baltimore) documents identical twin brothers who both carry the m.3243A>G MELAS mutation yet developed dramatically different neurological profiles — one experiencing stroke-like episodes, blurred vision, and learning difficulties from age 10; his brother first presenting with hearing loss at 12 before progressing to severe seizures. Both were treated with arginine, coenzyme Q10, levetiracetam, and ATP disodium, though with differing trajectories. The authors conclude that MELAS's extreme phenotypic variability — visible even between genetically identical individuals — frequently causes misdiagnosis or delayed diagnosis, and that early genetic testing is the critical intervention: presentations that don't match textbook criteria are still MELAS. The twins' mother was found to be an asymptomatic carrier at approximately 31% heteroplasmy. (According to PubMed: Wu, Wang & Kong, Medicine (Baltimore), May 2026 — doi:10.1097/MD.0000000000047264)
