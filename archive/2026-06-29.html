🧬 Mito Research
  • According to PubMed, a major review published June 26 in Biomedicine & Pharmacotherapy (Wang & Jhuang, Taipei Medical University) surveyed 159 clinical trials in primary mitochondrial diseases since 2010 and identified what the authors call an "emerging inflection point." For MELAS specifically, taurine has achieved regulatory authorization. Two additional therapies recently received FDA approval for related mitochondrial conditions: doxecitine/doxribtimine (Kygevvi) for TK2 deficiency, and elamipretide (FORZINITY) for Barth syndrome. Emerging strategies flagged as most promising include NAD⁺ augmentation, soluble guanylate cyclase stimulation (zagociguat, active Phase 2b MELAS trial), mTOR modulation, and heteroplasmy-targeting gene therapies. Roughly half of all PMD interventional trials have launched since 2020, with 50 studies currently active or recruiting. (Biomed Pharmacother — PubMed)
  • According to PubMed, a clinical letter published June 18 in Revista Española de Enfermedades Digestivas examined management of MELAS-associated chronic intestinal pseudo-obstruction (CIPO) in an m.3243A>G carrier, noting that despite colostomy, prokinetics, jejunal nutrition, and mitochondrial therapy, CIPO in MELAS is particularly refractory and carries a poor prognosis. A reminder of the severity of multi-system involvement at high heteroplasmy levels. (Rev Esp Enferm Dig — PubMed)
