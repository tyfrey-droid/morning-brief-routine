🇺🇸 US & Politics
  • The Supreme Court today ruled 6–3 to dramatically expand the president's power to remove heads of independent federal agencies, overturning the 91-year-old Humphrey's Executor precedent. Chief Justice Roberts wrote that the president cannot be "saddled with those with whom he cannot work." The ruling puts members of the FTC, EEOC, Merit Systems Protection Board, and Consumer Product Safety Commission in a more precarious position; the Fed's Lisa Cook will keep her seat for now under a narrow carve-out. Justice Sotomayor's dissent called the decision "grievously wrong." (NPR, CNN)
  • The Supreme Court ruled 6–3 on June 25 to let the Trump administration strip Temporary Protected Status from roughly 350,000 Haitians and 6,000 Syrians, clearing the legal path to deportation proceedings. Justice Alito's majority held that federal courts cannot review DHS decisions on whether to extend or terminate TPS designations. Justice Kagan's dissent accused the majority of minimizing racial undertones in Trump's prior public statements about Haitian immigrants. More than one million additional TPS holders from other countries now face compounding legal uncertainty. (NPR, NBC News)
  • President Trump canceled a scheduled signing ceremony for a new AI executive order today, telling reporters he did not want to sign anything that could "interfere with the U.S. competitive position" in artificial intelligence relative to China. The order's text was not released; no rescheduled signing has been announced. (White House)
  • Michigan Governor Gretchen Whitmer was among four Democratic governors who deployed their states' National Guard troops to Washington, D.C. ahead of America's 250th anniversary celebrations — a move that intersects with ongoing controversy over President Trump's extended use of active military forces in the capital. (PBS NewsHour)

✦ Faith & Theology
  • After an 80-year relationship, the United Methodist Church removed Asbury Theological Seminary from its list of approved ordination schools. The UMC's University Senate cited Asbury's traditional-marriage ethos as incompatible with the denomination's social principles and flagged the absence of a UMC faculty member teaching church history and polity. The 103-year-old Kentucky seminary — the largest Wesleyan institution in the U.S. — said students enrolled through fall 2026 will be grandfathered. (Christianity Today, Religion News Service)
  • At its 2026 General Assembly in Louisville, the Presbyterian Church in America voted overwhelmingly to reject Overture 37, which would have permitted local sessions to ordain women as deacons. The Overtures Committee recommended rejection 115–14; the full assembly agreed by voice vote, grounding the decision in the denomination's existing constitutional standards and reaffirming its complementarian position on church office. (Christian Post)
