🧬 Mito Research
  • A systematic review and meta-analysis of 988 MELAS patients published June 10 in Orphanet Journal of Rare Diseases confirms the m.3243A>G mutation in 78% of cases, with stroke-like episodes in virtually all patients, seizures in 89%, and elevated lactate in 81%. The temporal lobe is the most commonly affected region on imaging. The study maps core disease mechanisms — mitophagy, reactive oxygen species, and energy metabolism — and identifies L-arginine, PI3K-AKT-mTOR signaling, and the electron transport chain as the most tractable targets for multi-targeted therapeutic strategies. This is among the largest MELAS meta-analyses published. (Orphanet J Rare Dis — PubMed)
  • A comprehensive review in Cellular and Molecular Life Sciences (June 10) examines disease models for mitochondrial complex I deficiency — the mechanism underlying MELAS, Leigh syndrome, and LHON. The authors find existing mouse and cell-line models fail to capture tissue-specific heteroplasmy effects, and call for urgently needed human-relevant in vivo and in vitro models. The paper functions as a field-wide roadmap: until better models exist, translating lab findings to patients remains slow. (Cell Mol Life Sci — PubMed)
