🧬  Mito Research
  • A comprehensive review of emerging therapeutic strategies for mtDNA diseases — including MELAS and the m.3243A>G mutation — was published June 2 in Cell Reports Medicine by researchers at University College London and the MRC Mitochondrial Biology Unit at Cambridge. The paper surveys mitochondria-targeted nucleases, next-generation base editors that reduce mutant heteroplasmy, mitochondrial donation (replacement therapy), and mitochondrial transplantation. The authors are candid that disease-modifying therapies remain limited today, but highlight the pace of gene-editing advances as moving toward targeted intervention for specific pathogenic variants — including common MELAS mutations. This is one of the more useful recent summaries of the therapeutic pipeline for m.3243A>G patients. (Based on articles retrieved from PubMed — DOI: 10.1016/j.xcrm.2026.102841)
  • A parallel review from The Chinese University of Hong Kong, published May 28 in Molecular Biomedicine, covers how mitochondrial defects drive neurodegenerative disease across a broad spectrum of conditions and evaluates the current therapeutic landscape — from metabolic modulators and antioxidants through gene-targeted therapies and organelle replacement. Authors identify persistent challenges in clinical translation and emphasize the need for mechanism-informed combination therapies. (Based on articles retrieved from PubMed — DOI: 10.1186/s43556-026-00480-x)
