🧬  Mito Research
  • A case report published May 1 in Medicine documents twin brothers with MELAS carrying the m.3243A>G mutation who developed strikingly different clinical trajectories despite identical genetics — one with seizures and cognitive difficulties from age 10, the other with hearing loss followed by epilepsy at 12. Their mother is an asymptomatic carrier at ~31% heteroplasmy, underscoring how dramatically heteroplasmy level and tissue distribution shape phenotype. The paper discusses anticonvulsant management during status epilepticus and the mitochondrial supplement protocol used (coenzyme Q10, L-arginine, levetiracetam, ATP disodium), and calls for early genetic testing and family screening in suspected mitochondrial disease. (Medicine — PubMed, May 1, 2026)
