🧬 Mito Research
  • A case report published May 1 in Medicine (Baltimore) documents twin brothers with MELAS carrying the m.3243A>G mutation — the same variant as Emi — who presented with dramatically different symptom profiles despite identical genetics. The elder developed seizures, stroke-like episodes, and learning difficulties from age 10; the younger had hearing loss and refractory epilepsy from age 12. Their mother was an asymptomatic carrier at only 30% heteroplasmy, underscoring how widely phenotype can diverge even within a single family. Current management in the reported cases included levetiracetam for seizures, coenzyme Q10, L-carnitine, ATP disodium, and arginine — consistent with standard MELAS care. The authors emphasize that early genetic testing is critical to avoid misdiagnosis (both twins were initially treated for suspected viral encephalitis) and that family screening is warranted given maternal inheritance. (Wu et al., Medicine, May 2026 — via PubMed, DOI: 10.1097/MD.0000000000047264)
