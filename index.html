🌍 World
  • US-Iran fighting escalates as strikes widen. Since Tehran's ultimatum to reopen the Strait of Hormuz expired over the weekend, US Central Command launched a fourth wave of strikes on July 13 — using sea-based attack drones for the first time — hitting more than 300 Iranian military, radar and naval targets since President Trump declared the ceasefire "over" last week; oil prices have jumped roughly 10% since Friday on fears of prolonged Gulf shipping disruption. (Al Jazeera, CNN)
  • Russian barrage kills eight in Ukraine. (Al Jazeera)

Yulia Svyrydenko stepped down on July 12 as President Zelensky announced a cabinet reshuffle meant to sharpen Kyiv's foreign-policy and security operations; Svyrydenko, who had negotiated the US-Ukraine critical-minerals deal, was offered a new role overseeing relations with a key international partner. (Al Jazeera, CBS News)
  • Venezuela quake toll passes 4,300. The death toll from Venezuela's June 24 twin earthquakes has continued climbing sharply, reaching 4,333 confirmed dead as of July 11 according to National Assembly President Jorge Rodriguez, with more than 16,700 injured and roughly 19,000 people now living in displacement camps. (Bloomberg, CP24/CTV News)
  • Israel now controls nearly 70% of Gaza. Nine months into the ceasefire, Israeli forces control nearly 70% of the territory, up from about half when the truce began in October, according to an NPR analysis of Israeli statements and aid-access maps; expanding "restricted" zones have forced aid groups to suspend operations in parts of northern Gaza. (NPR)
